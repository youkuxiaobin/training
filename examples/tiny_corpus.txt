Language models learn by reading text and predicting the next token.
Small models are useful because they make the full training loop easy to inspect.
The tokenizer turns text into ids, the model predicts the next id, and the loss tells us how wrong it was.
Training starts noisy, then the model slowly learns repeated patterns in the corpus.
